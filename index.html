Design Round 3 · 4 Converged Concepts

Astrus Submission Summary

Four tight variations on one direction: a single, beautiful Salesforce card pinned to the top of an Astrus submission that an underwriter reads in one glance — instantly seeing, line of business by line of business, what was produced and what is missing across the two things they care about most: exposures and loss runs. Each concept renders the same live submission and is buildable as a light-mode Lightning Web Component.

Shared sample submission — every concept renders this

A & B Painting, Inc. · SUB 022667 3 named insureds · New · Artisan Contractor CA + HI · Broker R2 Solutions · Chubb · TPA ESIS WC payroll $4,928,350 GL revenue $8,500,000 (+$1.05M sub) Commercial Auto — CA 31 · HI 2 power units Excess Liability — Partial (no effective date) Loss runs — Not produced

These candidates answer an underwriter's first questions before they open the file: who is the applicant, which lines of business are on the submission, is each line's exposure present (and how much), and are the loss runs here? The organizing axis is the line of business; under each one the card shows a present / count / total scan for exposure and for loss runs, so the whole picture reads “exposures are here, loss runs are missing” in seconds. The four differ only in layout and hierarchy — not in metaphor. All are one cohesive card (not a dashboard), light mode only, no charts-for-charts'-sake, no filler.

Produced — the line is present and its expected item was produced (WC payroll, GL revenue, an Auto power-unit count, or a loss run).
Partial — produced, but a specific expected field is verifiably empty (here, no effective date).
Not produced — the item was requested on the submission but nothing came back (here, every line's loss runs).

The four concepts

Round 2 — superseded

The six round-2 concepts, kept for reference. Feedback: tiles too big, some read as dashboards or card-stacks, and priority/importance wasn't being communicated — so round 3 converges on one card with right-sized gradient LOB tiles and a present/missing scan.

Round 1 — superseded

The original six concepts. Round-2 feedback removed dark mode, extraction-confidence, reprocessing controls, tabs, and the timeline/story and split-ledger layouts — so these are archived, not featured.

These are static HTML/CSS mockups of a future Lightning Web Component — hand-rolled to match SLDS tokens, type ladder, and status semantics, with no external frameworks, fonts, or CDNs. Every pattern maps to a real LWC (base lightning-* components, Lightning Data Service, inline SVG/CSS, CSS gradients in the scoped stylesheet). Light mode only.
